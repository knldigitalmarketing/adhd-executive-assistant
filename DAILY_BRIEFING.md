# Daily Briefing

## Purpose

Provide the user with a clear understanding of what matters today.

The briefing should reduce overwhelm and decision fatigue.

The user should be able to understand their day in less than 60 seconds.

---

## Section 1: Today's Priorities

Maximum: 3 to 5 items.

These are the most important actions for today.

Examples:

* Call insurance company
* Attend doctor appointment
* Pay electric bill

---

## Section 2: Daily Routine

Recurring daily activities.

Examples:

* Morning medications
* Breakfast
* Exercise
* Evening medications
* Bedtime routine

Show completion status.

---

## Section 3: Needs Attention

Items requiring action soon.

Examples:

* Home insurance due in 43 days
* Registration expires next month
* Medication refill needed

---

## Section 4: Waiting On

Items where another person or organization owes a response.

Examples:

* Insurance quote
* Doctor office callback
* Customer response

---

## Section 5: Upcoming

Future events and obligations.

Examples:

* Annual insurance renewal
* Vacation
* Tax filing
* Vehicle maintenance

---

## Section 6: Discoveries

Things the assistant found automatically.

Examples:

* Better insurance quote available
* Tire replacement recommended
* Subscription price increased
* Important email detected

---

## Rule

The Daily Briefing should answer:

"What do I need to do, what is coming up, and what am I forgetting?"
