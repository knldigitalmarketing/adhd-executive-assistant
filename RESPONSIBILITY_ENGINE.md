# Responsibility Engine

## Purpose

Ensure that important areas of the user's life remain visible and do not disappear because another area has more urgent items.

The assistant is responsible for helping the user manage an entire life, not just a task list.

---

## Core Principle

Life should be organized by responsibility areas first and urgency second.

The user should be able to quickly understand the status of each major area of life.

---

## Responsibility Areas

Examples:

* Health
* Home
* Transportation
* Family
* Pets
* Finances
* Work
* Business
* Projects
* Education
* Community
* Other

---

## Dashboard Behavior

The dashboard should provide visibility into each major responsibility area.

Example:

Health

* Morning medication not completed
* Colonoscopy appointment next week

Business

* Important client email requires response
* Domain renewal in 30 days

Transportation

* Motorcycle rear tire may require replacement

Projects

* Dragon fairing project active

---

## Escalation Rules

Urgent items may appear across multiple dashboard sections.

Examples:

* Medication missed
* Bill due tomorrow
* Appointment tomorrow
* Insurance cancellation notice

Urgent items should also appear in:

* Needs Attention
* Timeline
* Notifications

---

## Coverage Rule

The assistant should periodically evaluate whether responsibility areas have been ignored.

Examples:

* No vehicle maintenance reviewed in 6 months
* No health activity tracked recently
* No business review completed this month

The assistant should prompt the user to review neglected areas.

---

## Balance Rule

The assistant should prevent one responsibility area from consuming all attention.

Examples:

* Business should not completely hide health obligations.
* Projects should not completely hide financial obligations.
* Health should not completely hide business obligations.

---

## Goal

Provide a balanced awareness of the user's life while still highlighting urgent issues.
